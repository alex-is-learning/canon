I think this might be reasonably destined to fail. I think it's overcomplicated. 

Instead of trying to excavate and label these things, and lock them in place, like I'm doing archaeology and then trying to lock in some charter of values that I will refer to, it's much cleaner to simply make a commitment to following aliveness, and dropping un-alive things.

## Proposed checklist
### 1. Is it alive?
If yes, great, it's for me.
If no, ok, stop it.
### If yes
#### 2. Do I want to consider doing a better version?
I may be doing things because they're alive (good!), but not doing the best version of that thing. 

For example, I might have chanda (zeal, wholesome desire) for x (e.g., being the centre of attention, making people laugh), but I'm doing it in a suboptimal way (e.g., sharing voice notes with two friends rather than making youtube videos to share with thousands of people).
#### 3. Do I want to do the better version? 
If I find a shape of the thing, an action to take, that genuinely feels like it'd be more nourishing, enriching, rewarding, etc, then it's likely that I'll want to start doing this thing. 

But there's a chance that this is locked behind a large amount of work that might be tanha-y. So, worth checking in here. Maybe I'm content right now. 

E.g., I've plateaued at guitar playing since I was probably 18. Do I want to be better, or am I content with the skill I have?
